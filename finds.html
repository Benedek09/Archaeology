Copper Fragments

AJ05 shows a collection of ornately decorated copper fragments of various size, which likely originate from the Victorian period. In this era, copper had many uses, such as cookware due to its antimicrobial properties.

Copper's reddish-brown hue also made it useful for decorative items in households, which is what these pieces were most probably used for.

Brooch

AJ09 is a beautiful brooch or badge, that features acorns in the design. The object looks as if it is iron - possibly cast - and is definitley metallic but worn.

This pin was likely worn on clothes and could date as far back as the roman or saxon period, as brooches were a popular item of self adornment then.

Bullet Casings

These are spent bullet casings, most of which are from .303 bullets. The .303 British is a 7.7mm calibre bullet that was the standard for British and Commonwealth rifles from 1889 to the 1950s.

They were most likely used during the Second World War and brought back by soldiers returning to their families as they are in relatively good condition.
